Explore Regions

Kargil

Jammu and Kashmir

About

Kargil is nestled in the Zaskar Range of the Himalayas. It is the site of border conflicts between India and Pakistan, including the Kargil War in 1999.

Description

Kargil has historical significance, dating back to the 16th century, blending Buddhist and Turkish architectural influences. Most of its residents are of Balti origin, and the majority follow the Shiʿi Muslim faith. Due to its proximity to the line of control, Kargil has been the site of border conflicts between India and Pakistan.

Hemkund Sahib

Uttarakhan

About

Hemkund Sahib is a Sikh pilgrimage site in Chamoli district. The glacial lake next to the gurdwara is pristine, earning it the name “Lake of Snow.”

Description

Situated at a breathtaking height of over 15,000 feet above sea level in the Himalayan ranges, Hemkund Sahib holds immense religious significance. According to the autobiographical account of the tenth Sikh Guru, Gobind Singh, it was at Hemkund, adorned with seven snow peaks, that he meditated in his previous birth.

Leh Ladakh

Jammu and Kashmir

About

Leh was the historical capital of the Kingdom of Ladakh. Leh serves as the gateway to the stunning Ladakh region, attracting adventure enthusiasts, trekkers, and spiritual seekers.

Description

Leh Ladakh is a region in northern India that captures the imagination with its dramatic landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and unique way of life. Nestled in the Himalayas, Leh, the largest town, serves as the gateway to this magical place.

North Sikkim

India

About

This largely uninhabited area shares its borders with China and Nepal, offering a mystical allure for travelers seeking rugged landscapes and vibrant meadows.

Description

North Sikkim is a crown jewel nestled in the Indian Himalayas, beckoning travelers with its breathtaking landscapes, rich cultural heritage, and serene ambiance. This region is a paradise for nature enthusiasts, adventure seekers, and anyone seeking a tranquil escape.
